Turn Based Games

These projects are a continuation of various skills, patterns, and ideas, originating from a simple concept: What if wizards played chess? As I dove deeper into turn based systems and altered mechanics, I explored tactics style games, and am actively working on a Tactics RPG which combines the techniques learned.

-Machine Learning and Utility AI
-Ability to simulate multiple turns ahead to evaluate current moves as done in traditional chess engines
-Pathfinding through implementation of A*
-Modular ability design for maximum code reusability and expansion
-Battle instances and overworlds
-Action and reaction queues similar to complex card games like Magic The Gathering
-Status effects and equipment stats

RTS: Battle Formations

This project started off loosely based on the RTS gameplay of Rome:Total War. The mechanics were experimental due to the twist of involving a personal “Hero” for the player. The main challenge faced in this was getting a comfortable and intuitive control system for the cohorts. AI profiling and optimization was important as the AI ended up getting quite complex. This project had to be paused due to a lack of funding to continue full time development.

-Utility AI on a per-unit level.
-Advancements in unit placements to reinforce allies
-Advanced pathfinding including obstacle avoidance steering
-Heavy optimization due to the number of AI agents

Project Voxel

This project involved creating a game around voxel destruction prior to any existing games with this feature hitting the market. It started off as a technology demo, and the goal was to make something out of it. Performance was the primary challenge of this project throughout. This project was cut short by a job offer with non-compete clause. While it was interesting at the time, since this was made, more efficient voxel destruction engines have been made and it has lost its novelty.

-Custom importer for Magica Voxel files into Unity
-Mesh destruction and rebuilds, allowing for the revealing of inner parts such as wires and studs inside walls
-Different defense values for different colour voxels, allowing for different materials in a given object.
-Top Down Shooter gameplay, later first person shooter gameplay
-Voxel objects as full physics objects
-Custom Shaders for custom voxel objects and small object cleanup.

Action RPGs

This was a large project aiming to produce an adventure experience which mixes Zelda and Metroid. It had a great deal of love and care toward the character controller, combat, AI, and broader game design, however it suffered from extreme overscoping. When my artist moved on to another position, I reevaluated scope and thought it best to pivot to something smaller. My favourite part of this project was the work on the procedural levels, which never left their respective branches, but were seeming quite promising. I have since done a couple smaller projects of similar style for fun.

-Implemented AI with Finite State Machines and Utility AI for various enemy types.
-Complex animation controllers
-Procedural animations and IK for animations such as ledge climbing, vaulting, and object carrying.
-Common action adventure features such as combo attacks, climbing, dodging, swimming
-Unlockable abilities for Metroidvania style design.
-Generic level triggers for things such as cutscenes, doors and locks, or environment alterations.
-Inventory system and loot
-Procedural level generation using a mix of rulebased design and cellular automata.
-Custom art, animation, and lore
